Lesson 1
A Wrong Man in Workers’ Paradise

Rabindranath Tagore

A Wrong Man in Workers' Paradise is a beautiful allegorical story by Rabindranath Tagore. It contrasts a society devoted only to work with the importance of imagination, creativity and beauty. The artist in the story transforms the lives of the people by introducing art into a world that values only usefulness.

II. COMPREHENSION QUESTIONS

A. Answer briefly the following questions.

1. “The man indulged in mad whims.” Who considers the man's work as "mad whims?"

  • a) The man himself
  • b) The writer
  • c) The people in Workers’ Paradise ✅

Answer: The people in Workers' Paradise considered his work as "mad whims" because they believed only useful work had value.

2. Explain the comparison in paragraph 3.

Comparison Answer
Some boys The man on Earth
They are not studying The man doing no useful work
Yet passing in the test The man entering Heaven

3. When the men say, "We haven't a moment to spare," it means—

  • a) They are overburdened.
  • b) They are proud to be busy. ✅
  • c) They are indifferent.

Answer: The workers took pride in remaining busy all the time.

4. Why is the torrent in Workers' Paradise silent?

Answer: The torrent is silent because everything in Workers' Paradise is expected to work efficiently. Even nature is not allowed to express beauty freely.

5. "The girl's hair was carelessly done." This indicates—

  • a) She was interested only in work. ✅
  • b) She disliked dressing her hair.
  • c) She had no sense of dressing.

Answer: She spent all her time working and never cared about beauty or appearance.

6. Why did the girl of the silent torrent feel sorry for the man?

Answer: She pitied him because he had no work and seemed to have no purpose in life.

7. "The girl scanned the painted pot at home secretly." This line indicates—

  • a) She was impressed by art and beauty. ✅
  • b) She wanted to hide it from the artist.
  • c) She feared the elders.

Answer: She secretly admired the beauty of the painted pot although society discouraged such appreciation.

8. "The hurrying feet" of the girl became "less hurried" because

  • a) She became lazy like the man.
  • b) She was attracted by art.
  • c) She was trying to re-arrange priorities.
  • d) The meaningless was slowly becoming meaningful. ✅

Answer:
The girl gradually began to appreciate beauty and art. She realized that life is not only about work but also about creativity and happiness.

9. Why did the elders of the Workers' Paradise become anxious?

Answer:
The elders became anxious because the wrong man's influence spread among the people. They began to enjoy painting, beauty and art instead of working all the time. This threatened the discipline of Workers' Paradise.

10. The girl follows the man out of the Workers' Paradise. This suggests that

  • a) She was bored with Workers' Paradise.
  • b) She was fascinated by his painting.
  • c) She saw new possibilities opening before her. ✅

Answer:
The girl understood that life should include beauty, imagination and freedom. Therefore she chose a new way of living.

11. What does the line "The man never believed in mere utility" mean?

Answer:
The man believed that life is not meant only for useful work. Beauty, imagination, creativity and art are equally important because they bring joy and meaning to life.

12. What changes occur in the girl's behaviour or attitude at the following stages?

Stage Change in Behaviour
Before giving her pitcher She believed only work was important.
After giving her pitcher She became curious about beauty and art.
When the man offered ribbons She began to enjoy beauty and self-expression.
When she followed him She completely accepted a life filled with art, beauty and freedom.

13. At the end of the story, there is a complete change in the girl's attitude. Who deserves the credit?

Answer:
Both deserve the credit. The wrong man awakened the hidden love for beauty through his artistic talent. The girl, however, had an open mind and was willing to change. Without her readiness, the artist's influence would not have transformed her life.

B. CLOSE STUDY

Read the following extracts carefully. Discuss in pairs and then write the answers to the questions given below them.

1. "But the Moving Finger writes even in heaven."

a) What does "Moving Finger" mean here?

Answer: It refers to the power of fate or destiny that records and decides the course of events. Even Heaven cannot escape destiny.

b) What figure of speech is used in "Moving Finger"?

Answer: Personification (also interpreted as Metonymy). The "Moving Finger" represents fate as if it were a living being that writes.

c) What does the sentence mean in the context?

Answer: It means that even in Heaven unexpected events can happen. The arrival of the wrong man shows that destiny works everywhere.

2. "As a princess sees a lonely beggar and is filled with pity, so the busy girl of heaven was filled with pity."

a) Who is compared to a lonely beggar?

Answer: The wrong man (the artist) is compared to a lonely beggar.

b) Why was the girl filled with pity?

Answer: She believed he had no useful work and therefore no purpose in life.

c) How did the girl offer to help him?

Answer: She offered to give him some work so that he could become useful.

3. "When she set out for the torrent the next day, her hurrying feet were a little less hurried than before."

a) What had happened the previous night?

Answer: She secretly admired the beautiful painting on her pitcher and began appreciating art.

b) What does "hurrying feet" suggest?

Answer: It suggests her busy life that was completely devoted to work and efficiency.

c) Why did they become less hurried?

Answer: The beauty created by the artist changed her attitude. She started valuing art, imagination and happiness along with work.

III. PARAGRAPH WRITING

Discuss the answers to the following questions in pairs or groups of four. Individually note down the important points for each question and then develop them into paragraph answers.

1. Men in Workers' Paradise say, "We haven't a moment to spare." The artist says, "I haven't a moment to spare for work." Contrast their attitudes.

Answer:

The two statements represent two completely different attitudes towards life. The people in Workers' Paradise believe that every moment should be spent in useful work. They consider hard work to be the only purpose of life and take pride in being constantly busy. On the other hand, the artist believes that life is incomplete without beauty, imagination and creativity. He spends his time creating art instead of doing mechanical work. While the workers value productivity, the artist values self-expression. Tagore suggests that a balanced life should include both work and creativity.

2. There are two worlds in the story: the world of the artist and the world of the Workers' Paradise. Which world is better? Why?

Answer:

Both worlds have their own strengths. Workers' Paradise is disciplined, organized and productive, but it lacks joy, beauty and imagination. The artist's world celebrates creativity, freedom and happiness but cannot function without some amount of useful work. Therefore, the ideal world is one that combines both. Human beings need hard work for survival, but they also need art, music, beauty and creativity to make life meaningful.

3. Debate: Aesthetics Vs Utility

Points for Discussion

  • ✔ Art for the sake of art.
  • ✔ Knowledge for the sake of knowledge.
  • ✔ Art enriches human life.
  • ✔ Knowledge develops wisdom.
  • ✘ Art only for making money.
  • ✘ Knowledge only for getting a job.
  • ✔ Man is not satisfied with bread alone.

Conclusion:

A balanced society values both utility and aesthetics. Utility provides the necessities of life, while art and knowledge provide happiness, culture and emotional satisfaction. Tagore reminds us that people need both work and beauty to live a complete life.

C. SUB-SKILLS OF READING

The purpose of reading determines the way we read a passage. Reading for the main idea is called Skimming. Reading for specific information is called Scanning.

i) Skimming

Read the following news item carefully.

A Korean Airbus with 199 passengers crashed 5 km away from Tripoli Airport in Libya and burst into flames, killing at least 100 people. The official Libyan News Agency reported that the aircraft fell on two houses. The crash occurred at 7:00 a.m., about 25 minutes before the scheduled landing.

Select the most appropriate heading.

  1. Hundred feared killed in plane crash ✅
  2. Plane crashes before landing
  3. Tragedy near Tripoli
  4. Captain survives plane crash
  5. Plane falls on houses
  6. Airbus in flames

Answer

Correct Answer: Hundred feared killed in plane crash

This heading best summarizes the main idea of the news report because the most important information is the heavy loss of life.

VI. SPEAKING ACTIVITIES

A. Role Play

Look at the conversations in the story between the girl and the man from:

  • Paragraphs 11 – 19
  • Paragraphs 20 – 26
  • Paragraphs 30 – 35

Work in pairs. One student takes the role of The Man and the other takes the role of The Girl. Read the dialogues aloud with proper expression and voice modulation.

Segment 1 : The First Encounter (Paragraphs 11–19)

Man : (Sighing) Work! I've not a moment to spare for work.
Girl : A-ha! You have no work in hand, have you?
Girl : I shall spare some work for you to do, if you like.
Man : Girl of the silent torrent, all this time I have been waiting to take some work from your hands.
Girl : What kind of work would you like?
Man : Will you give me one of your pitchers, one that you can spare?
Girl : A pitcher? You want to draw water from the torrent?
Man : No. I shall draw pictures on your pitcher.
Girl : Pictures indeed! I have no time to waste on such as you. I'm going.

Segment 2 : Persistence and Discovery (Paragraphs 20–26)

Man : Girl of the silent torrent, give me one of your clay pitchers. I shall draw pictures on it.
Girl : (After giving the pitcher) What do they mean, all those lines and colours? What is their purpose?
Man : Nothing. A picture may have no meaning and serve no purpose.

Segment 3 : The Ribbon and Transformation (Paragraphs 30–35)

Girl : What do you want of me?
Man : Only some more work from your hands.
Girl : What kind of work would you like?
Man : Let me make a coloured ribbon for your hair.
Girl : And what for?
Man : Nothing.

🎭 Tips for Role Play

  • The Girl: Begin with a busy, impatient and serious tone. Gradually become curious, thoughtful and cheerful.
  • The Man: Speak calmly, gently and confidently. Show creativity, patience and imagination throughout the conversation.
  • Maintain eye contact with your partner.
  • Use proper voice modulation and expressions.
  • Read the dialogue naturally and confidently.

B. Making Requests

"The man asked the girl, Will you give me one of your pitchers?" This is a polite request.

There are several polite ways of making requests.

  • Would/Could you give me ____________?
  • Will you please give me ____________?
  • Would / Do you mind if I use your ____________?

Activity

Work in pairs and make suitable requests for the following situations.

1. Your car breaks down in the middle of a busy road.

You stop a biker and his friend and politely request them to help you push the car to the side.

Sample Answers

Option 1:
Excuse me, my car has broken down. Could you please help me push it to the side of the road?

Option 2:
Would you mind helping me move my car to the roadside?

Option 3:
Could you spare a few minutes to help me push my car? I would be very grateful.

2. Request your Headmaster for 15 days' leave.

Your grandfather is critically ill and you wish to stay with him.

Sample Answers

Option 1:
Respected Headmaster, I request you to kindly grant me 15 days' leave as my grandfather is critically ill.

Option 2:
Would you kindly grant me 15 days' leave? I need to stay with my grandfather during this difficult time.

Option 3:
Could you please consider my request for 15 days' leave? I assure you that I will complete all my pending work after returning.

💡 Tips for Making Polite Requests

  • Always begin with polite words such as Please, Excuse me, or Kindly.
  • Use expressions like Could you..., Would you..., or Would you mind....
  • Thank the person after making the request.
  • Speak respectfully and politely.

D. Conversation

Peter plans to spend a week in Ooty with his family. He approaches a travel agent to make arrangements for the trip. Read the following conversation and role-play it with your partner.

Conversation between Peter and the Travel Agent

Travel Agent: Hello Sir, welcome to the Rainbow Travel Agency. What can I do for you?
Peter: I am planning to take my family to Ooty for a one-week holiday next month. I would like to make all the travel and accommodation arrangements.
Travel Agent: You have come to the right place, Sir. We specialize in family tours and provide the best travel, sightseeing and accommodation facilities.
Peter: Wonderful! I want comfortable accommodation near the important tourist places.
Travel Agent: Certainly, Sir. May I know how many days you plan to stay?
Peter: We are planning to stay for one week.
Travel Agent: Excellent. We provide sightseeing by private vehicle. You can visit Ooty Lake, Botanical Garden, Doddabetta Peak, Tea Museum and many other beautiful places.
Peter: Yes, we don't want a hurried visit. We want to spend quality time there. My children are interested in boating and horse riding.
Travel Agent: Certainly, Sir. They will enjoy boating at Ooty Lake and horse riding near the gardens. We also have excellent lodging facilities available during the second week of April.
Peter: That sounds perfect. Please book the package for my family. Can I pay by cash or credit card?
Travel Agent: Yes Sir. We accept both cash and credit cards.
Peter: Thank you very much.
Travel Agent: Thank you, Sir. Hope your stay in Ooty is enjoyable and unforgettable.

It's Never Too Late

Read the passage carefully and observe the correct use of Articles and Prepositions.

East Taiwan consists of Hualien and Taitung counties. The area covers 8,143 square kilometers, about one-fifth of Taiwan's total area, but has a population of only 610,000 or just three percent of the population of Taiwan. Communities are usually found in the few scattered flat areas. The administration centres, Hualien City and Taitung City, are located in the north and south of the area respectively.

Apart from these two cities, there are more than twenty towns and villages scattered throughout the area, so you will not find yourself alone while travelling.

East Taiwan faces the Pacific Ocean and is bounded on the west by the Central Range. A 170-km-long Coastal Range bisects the region, running parallel to the coast.

The total length of East Taiwan's coastline is over 300 kilometres. Numerous scenic places are to be found in this exceptional geographical environment. The region also encompasses two islands lying just off the coast opposite Taitung County, Green Island and Orchid Island.

These tiny islands, only 16 square kilometres and 46 square kilometres respectively, have some marvellous scenery both below and above the high-water mark.

💡 Grammar Tips

  • The is used before specific places such as the Pacific Ocean and the Central Range.
  • A is used before a singular countable noun mentioned for the first time.
  • In indicates position or location.
  • By indicates proximity or agency (bounded by).
  • To is used with the expression parallel to.
  • Off means at a short distance from the coast.
  • Below and above describe positions relative to the high-water mark.
