II. COMPREHENSION QUESTIONS

A. Answer briefly the following questions

1. The time of the day suggested in the poem is

Answer: a. Sunrise

The poem suggests sunrise because the bird appears to pull the reluctant sun from the horizon.

2. The sun is described as the reluctant sun. It means that,

Answer: c. It was the bird's feeling that the sun was reluctant to rise.

3(a). In the first four lines, there is exaggeration in the description. What is it?

Answer:

The exaggeration is that the bird appears to pull the reluctant sun out from the horizon. This beautifully highlights the importance of the bird's morning call.

3(b). What is the figure of speech used here?

Answer: Hyperbole

Hyperbole is used because the poet deliberately exaggerates the bird's action.

4. How was the majestic neck humbled by the hunter?

Answer:

The hunter killed the male crane. Its proud and majestic neck became lifeless and was stuffed into a coarse washing bag like dirty linen.

5. The expression "picked up hands and jaws" suggests

Answer: d. Cruelty of the hunters

6. ____________ is compared to "dirty linen".

Answer: a. The proud neck of the bird

7. "And sat to hatch the blood-stained feathers into a toddling chick." This suggests

Answer: d. The desperate act of the female bird to bring the male bird back to life.

8. How is the end of the female crane suggested in the poem?

Answer:

The female crane could not bear the grief of losing her mate. She finally died of sorrow and went beyond the limits of human understanding, symbolizing eternal love and reunion with her partner.

B. CLOSE STUDY

Read the following extract carefully.

A wave of the seas she had never seen
came to her from far away
and carried her to him.

a. What does ‘wave of the seas’ refer to?

Answer:

The phrase "wave of the seas" is a metaphor for death. Just as a powerful sea wave carries everything away, death carried the female crane away from the physical world and reunited her with her beloved partner.

b. What hadn’t the female bird seen before?

Answer:

The female crane had never seen the sea before. This suggests that death came from a world beyond her own experience and carried her to her mate.

c. What figure of speech is used in the extract?

Answer:

The figure of speech used is Metaphor. The poet compares death to a powerful sea wave that carries the female crane away.

III. PARAGRAPH WRITING

Discuss in pairs/groups of four each and answer the following questions. Individually, note down the points for each question and then develop the points into one-paragraph answers.

1. How is the callousness of the bird-killers brought out in the poem?

Key Points

  • The male crane was peacefully welcoming the sunrise.
  • The hunters killed the bird without any reason.
  • The proud neck was treated like dirty linen.
  • The hunters showed no sympathy or respect for nature.
  • They left the grieving female crane behind.

Paragraph Answer

The poet brings out the callousness of the bird-killers by showing how they mercilessly kill a beautiful and innocent crane. The bird was peacefully welcoming the sunrise when it was suddenly shot. Its proud neck was treated like dirty linen and stuffed into a coarse washing bag. The hunters showed no sympathy for the suffering of the female crane and walked away without any regret. Through these images, the poet strongly condemns human cruelty towards nature.

2. How does the poet bring out the agony and desperation of the female crane in the poem?

Key Points

  • The female crane cried loudly after her mate was killed.
  • She flew repeatedly around the place of death.
  • She could not accept the loss of her partner.
  • She tried to hatch the blood-stained feathers.
  • She finally died of grief.

Paragraph Answer

The poet vividly describes the unbearable grief of the female crane. After the death of her mate, she cried continuously and circled the place where he was killed. She could not accept his death and even attempted to hatch the blood-stained feathers as if she hoped to bring him back to life. Her sorrow became so intense that she finally died and joined her beloved mate. This shows the depth of love and lifelong companionship between the pair.

3. Pick out any two figures of speech used in the poem and explain how they add to the effectiveness of the poem.

Key Points

  • Hyperbole: "Pull the reluctant sun out."
  • Metaphor: "Dirty linen."
  • They create vivid mental pictures.
  • They make the poem more emotional and meaningful.

Paragraph Answer

The poet uses several figures of speech to make the poem powerful. The hyperbole "pull the reluctant sun out" exaggerates the crane's beauty and importance in nature. The metaphor "dirty linen" compares the dead crane's neck to something worthless, highlighting the cruelty of the hunters. These figures of speech make the poem more expressive, emotional and memorable for the readers.
