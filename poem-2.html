II. COMPREHENSION QUESTIONS

A. Answer the following questions briefly

1. In the first line, 'he' refers to the son; and 'I' refers to Abraham Lincoln (the father).

2. "He will have to learn" means that

  • Answer: b. The son has no choice.

3. "All men are not just, all men are not true."

  • Answer: b. The harsh reality of life.

4. "For every scoundrel there is a hero" means

  • Answer: c. In spite of all the wickedness in this world, there is enough goodness.

5. Why is it important for a child to learn that the world is also filled with heroes, dedicated leaders and friends?

It helps the child develop a balanced outlook. Although evil exists in the world, goodness, honesty and friendship also exist. This gives hope and inspires him to become a good human being.

6. The first seven lines emphasize

  • Answer: c. Both the positive and the negative sides of life.

7. Pick out the line which says that hard earned money has greater value than easy money.

"That a dollar earned is of far more value than five found."

8. What does the phrase "learn to lose" mean?

It means learning to accept defeat gracefully and understanding that failure is a part of life.

9. In what sense is it more honorable to fail than to cheat?

Failure with honesty preserves one's character, while cheating destroys integrity and self-respect.

10(a). Why doesn't the father want his son to follow the crowd?

He wants his son to think independently and stand for what is right instead of blindly following others.

10(b). How can this value be linked to having faith in oneself?

A person with self-confidence trusts his own judgment and is not influenced by public opinion.

11. What kind of a listener does the father want his son to be?

He should listen to everyone but filter everything through the "screen of truth" before accepting it.

12(a). Is it possible to laugh when one is sad?

Yes. It shows emotional strength and courage.

12(b). What value is highlighted?

Emotional maturity, resilience and self-control.

13. What does Lincoln mean by "too much sweetness"?

He refers to false praise, flattery and insincere behaviour.

14. What does "close his ears to a howling mob" mean?

It means not yielding to public pressure or following people blindly.

15. What is the ultimate teaching Lincoln recommends?

He wants his son to have sublime faith in himself so that he will also have faith in mankind.

16. What does "not to put a price tag on his soul" mean?

Never compromise honesty, values or character for money or personal gain.

17. Why is it essential to have "sublime faith in mankind"?

Faith in humanity helps us remain hopeful, kind and positive despite difficulties.

18(a). What does "this" refer to?

It refers to all the values Lincoln asks the teacher to teach his son.

18(b). What does "a big order" mean?

It means a very difficult task.

19. "This is a big order" implies

  • Answer: c. The task of teaching such values is too difficult for any teacher or school.

20. Expressions showing the task is difficult:

  • "If you can"
  • "It will take time, I know."
  • "But see what you can do."

21. Contrasting values in the poem:

  • Enemy × Friend
  • Lose × Win
  • Fail × Cheat
  • Gentle × Tough
  • Patience × Impatience

22. Identify the lines highlighting the following values.

Value Line
Positive attitude "For every scoundrel there is a hero."
Accept failure "Teach him to learn to lose."
Wonder of Nature "Ponder the eternal mystery of birds, bees and flowers."
Faith in oneself "Teach him to have sublime faith in himself."
Independent thinking "Not to follow the crowd."
Not being gullible "Filter all he hears on a screen of truth."
Right and wrong "It is far more honourable to fail than to cheat."

B. CLOSE STUDY

Read the following extracts carefully. Discuss in pairs and then write the answers to the questions given below them.

Extract 1

"Teach him that for every enemy there is a friend.
It will take time, I know;"

a) What does the first line mean?

The line means that for every person who acts as an enemy, there is also someone who is a friend. It teaches the son not to lose hope because goodness and friendship always exist alongside hatred.

b) What human virtue is highlighted here?

The virtue highlighted is optimism and faith in the goodness of humanity.

c) What will take time?

It will take time for the son to understand and practise these moral values and life lessons.

Extract 2

"Only the test of fire makes fine steel."

a) Whom is this line meant for?

The line is meant for Lincoln's son. The father requests the teacher to help his son become strong in character.

b) Explain the literal meaning of the line.

Steel becomes strong and pure only after passing through intense fire. Similarly, a person becomes stronger after facing difficulties and challenges.

c) What human virtue is emphasized here?

The line emphasizes resilience, courage, perseverance, determination and inner strength.

III. PARAGRAPH WRITING

Discuss in pairs/groups of four each, the answers to the following questions. Individually, note down the important points for each question and then develop the points into one-paragraph answers.

1. “Teach him to listen to all men but teach him to filter all he hears on a screen of truth.”

Key Points

  • Open-mindedness – Listen respectfully to everyone.
  • Critical thinking – Do not believe everything blindly.
  • Discernment – Separate truth from falsehood.
  • Wisdom – Accept only what is right and useful.
  • Independent thinking – Make decisions based on truth.

Paragraph Answer

These lines highlight the importance of balancing open-mindedness with critical thinking. Abraham Lincoln wants his son to listen patiently to every person because everyone has something valuable to share. However, he also advises him not to accept everything without thinking. The "screen of truth" symbolizes wisdom and good judgment, helping him distinguish truth from lies, useful advice from harmful opinions, and honesty from deception. By following this principle, the son will become a wise, responsible and independent thinker who always chooses the right path.

2. Do you agree with the poet when he says, “Only the test of fire makes fine steel”? Give reasons to support your point of view.

Key Points

  • Life's challenges build character.
  • Difficulties develop courage and confidence.
  • Failures teach valuable lessons.
  • Hard work and perseverance lead to success.
  • Challenges make a person stronger and wiser.

Paragraph Answer

I completely agree with the poet's statement, "Only the test of fire makes fine steel." Steel becomes strong only after passing through intense heat. Likewise, people become mentally strong, confident and successful only after facing hardships and challenges. Difficult situations teach patience, determination and resilience. They also help us learn from our mistakes and improve ourselves. A life without struggles does not prepare us for future responsibilities. Therefore, difficulties are essential for building a strong character and achieving success in life.
